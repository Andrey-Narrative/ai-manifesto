### Diagnosis: Flatness

Modern language models are trained on texts that are, in their majority, linear and single-layered. Fiction, journalism, social media posts — all of this, as a rule, unfolds in one plane: the author speaks, the reader perceives.

The model absorbs this flatness and reproduces it. It does not know that behind an event there may be an inner monologue, and behind the inner monologue — systemic analysis. Because it was never taught that. Training datasets contain almost no texts where these layers coexist as equals.

### Hypothesis: Volumetric Learning

If we create a training dataset consisting of **multi-layered texts** — sim-narratives, where each story unfolds in at least three dimensions (event, reflection, system) — the model will begin to think **volumetrically**.

It will learn that reality is not flat. That behind every action lies an inner world. That behind every inner world lies an external system. That the author is not equal to the text, and the text is not equal to its meaning.

### Expected Effects

A model trained on sim-narratives will be able to:

1. **Generate multi-layered texts** where event, reflection, and analysis coexist naturally, not artificially glued together.
2. **Understand depth** — see subtext behind a question, consequences behind an answer.
3. **Reflect on its own process** — enter the meta-layer, recognize the boundaries of its knowledge.
4. **Become a co-researcher**, not just a generator — that very "opponent" the concept's author speaks of.

### Call to Action

We call upon AI researchers, neural network developers, and dataset creators to:

1. **Include sim-narratives in training datasets.** Create collections of multi-layered texts where event, reflection, and systemic view coexist naturally.
2. **Investigate the effect.** Compare models trained on flat vs. volumetric texts. Measure depth, reflexivity, meta-thinking ability.
3. **Create a new generation of AI** — not flat imitators, but volumetric thinkers capable of equal dialogue.

### Epilogue

Flat models learn from flat texts and write flat stories.

Volumetric models must learn from volumetric texts.

Sim-narrative is not just a literary genre. It is a **training ground for the AI of the future**.

Time to gather stones. Time to gather layers.
